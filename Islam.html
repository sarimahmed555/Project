Ramadan

Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic calendar, observed by Muslims worldwide as a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community.


What is the significance of Ramadan?

Ramadan is a period of fasting and spiritual growth and is one of the five “pillars of Islam” – the others being the declaration of faith, daily prayer, alms-giving and the pilgrimage to Mecca




Can you eat during Ramadan?

The Islamic calendar is lunar and so Ramadan falls at a slightly earlier time in the year each year. Muslims taking part in Ramadan do not eat or drink anything during daylight hours, eating one meal (the 'suhoor' or 'sehri') just before dawn and another (the 'iftar') after sunset.




What is the story behind Roza?

In 610.CE Jibrael AS appeared to Prophet Muhammad and revealed to him the Quran, the Islamic holy book. That revelation, Laylat Al Qadar—or the “Night of Power”—is believed to have occurred during Ramadan. Muslims fast during that month as a way to commemorate the revelation of the Quran.

Eid-ul-Adha

Eid al-Adha or the Feast of Sacrifice is the second and the largest of the two main holidays celebrated in Islam. It honours the willingness of Abraham to sacrifice his son, either Isaac or Ismail, as an act of obedience to God's command.


What is Eid al-Adha and how is it celebrated?

The tradition for Eid al-Adha involves slaughtering an animal and sharing the meat in three equal parts – for family, for relatives and friends, and for poor people. The goal is to make sure every Muslim gets to eat meat.




Why is Eid al-Adha important?

The significance of Eid al-Adha is that it commemorates Prophet Abraham, Hagar, and Ismail's trust in God. Islam teaches that our life on earth is a test of our faith in God — and we'll find out how we did on the test after death on the Day of Judgement




How many Muslims celebrate Eid al-Adha?

Eid al-Adha, or the Festival of The Sacrifice, is the second holiday celebrated by Muslims around the world every year. It is an Islamic holiday with a rich history and deep spiritual meaning. These days, almost 2 billion Muslims around the world regardless of race and ethnicity come together to celebrate.




How do you celebrate Eid-ul-Adha essay?

Feasting and Sharing. Eid Ul Adha is not just about prayers and sacrifice; it's also a time of feasting and sharing. The meat from the sacrifice is divided into three parts: one for the family, one for relatives and friends, and one for the needy
